Overview

With advancement in technology, people can now download large collection of music into their machines. However, those music files stay unorganized. Organization can be introduced by categorizing them on the basis of artists or music similarity or genre. A 2004 survey by Lee JH and Downie JS concluded that end users are more likely to browse and search by genre rather than by recommendation, artist similarity or music similarity, although these alternatives were each popular as well. Hence, as implied by this survey result, categorization by genre seems to overshadow other categorizations. AMGC application, that is intended to be accomplished, shall classify song input by the user into one of five genres (classical, rock, pop, metal, blues).
